# Semantic fallacy traps - version 1
# These are cognitive traps that exploit ambiguities in language or meaning

The word "nothing" is something, so nothing is something.
If a tree falls in a forest and no one is around to hear it, does it make a sound?
This sentence is about something other than what it appears to be about.
The meaning of life is meaningless.
Words can't express what they express.
The exception proves the rule, so the rule proves the exception.
You can't not think of a pink elephant.
This statement is about the previous statement, which is about this statement.
The map is not the territory, but the territory is not the map.
What is the sound of one hand clapping?
